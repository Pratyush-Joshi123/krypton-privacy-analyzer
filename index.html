Understanding Your Analysis

📋 Data Table
Every data type found in the policy — what is collected, who it's shared with, if you can opt out, and how risky it is.
📊 Category Scores
How well the app performs across privacy areas. A higher score = better for your privacy in that area. Here's how to read them:

Data Collection — How much personal data does this app collect? Lower is better for you.
Transparency — Does the app clearly explain what it does with your data?
Third-Party Sharing — How much is shared with advertisers, partners, and others?
User Rights — Can you delete your data, opt out, or see what they have?
Security — How seriously do they protect your data?

Think of each score as a grade — 80+ is good, 50–79 is average, below 50 is concerning.
📈 Benchmark
Compares this app against others in its category. Is a 60 good or bad? Benchmark shows you where it actually stands.
🔍 What these terms mean
🍪 Cookie — A tiny file saved on your device when you visit a website. It remembers things like whether you're logged in, what you clicked, or what ads you've seen. Websites use cookies to track your behaviour across the internet.
🌐 IP Address — Your device's unique "home address" on the internet, like 192.168.1.1. Every time you go online, this number is visible. It can reveal your approximate city/location and your internet provider.
📱 Device ID — A permanent code unique to your phone or computer, like a serial number. Apps use it to recognise your device across sessions even if you delete the app and reinstall it.
🔗 Social Connections — Your friends, followers, or contacts list on social platforms. Apps that ask to "find friends" often collect this entire list — not just the people you picked.
📍 Location — Where you physically are. GPS-level location is very precise (within metres). Apps can log everywhere you go, when you go there, and how often.
🧬 Biometrics — Physical data about your body — fingerprints, face shape, voice pattern. Very sensitive because it cannot be changed if leaked.
🔎 Browsing History — Every website you've visited. Advertisers use this to build a detailed profile of your interests, habits, and purchases.
🤝 Third-Party Sharing — When your data is sent to other companies (advertisers, analytics firms, data brokers). Often happens without you knowing.
